  • Warehouse Management System

    Warehouse Management System (WMS) is a software application that helps manage and control warehouse operations.

    • Inventory Management: WMS tracks the movement and storage of inventory items within the warehouse. It provides real-time visibility into inventory levels, locations, and status.
    • Order Management: WMS manages the processing of orders, including order picking, packing, and shipping. It optimizes order fulfillment processes to improve efficiency and accuracy.
    • Warehouse Layout Optimization: WMS helps optimize warehouse layout and storage strategies to maximize space utilization and minimize travel time for picking operations.
    • Inventory Tracking: WMS uses barcode or RFID technology to track inventory items throughout the warehouse, ensuring accurate inventory counts and reducing the risk of stockouts or overstock.
    • Labor Management: WMS helps manage warehouse workforce by assigning tasks, tracking productivity, and optimizing labor resources to improve efficiency.
    • Reporting and Analytics: WMS provides reports and analytics on warehouse performance, including inventory levels, order fulfillment rates, and labor productivity, to help make informed decisions.

  • Unknown Products

    Unknown Products are the goods that are not recognized or identified within the inventory system. Items that are mislabeled, damaged, or have unclear markings, which make it difficult for warehouse staff to understand their identity or origin are also considered as unknown products. Unknown products are managed by isolating them from known inventory, attempting to identify them by checking labels or packaging details, recording these details for future reference, and storing them.

  • Unique Purchase Orders, Items & Quantities by month. Comparing Q-o-Q, Y-o-Y

    The analysis of unique purchase orders, items, and quantities by month involves tracking and understanding the flow of goods entering and exiting the warehouse over time.This analysis helps in identifying patterns, trends, and fluctuations in demand, which is crucial for optimizing inventory management and operational efficiency.

  • Shipment Reconciliation

    Shipment reconciliation is the process of matching actual inventory against booked inventory.Reconciliation occurs only when order items are fully received. Until reconciliation, inventory is not eligible for picking and remains in the warehouse but not allocated space. Shipment reconciliation ensures that inventory becomes eligible for picking.

  • Hold - Recalled goods. Product Quality, Damaged products

    A status applied to inventory items that temporarily restricts their movement or processing within the warehouse. It is to be done if you want product unavailable for sale. To manage holds, machine-learning algorithms can be employed to analyze historical data on hold reasons, durations, and outcomes.

Kitting can help companies generate higher revenue and increase operating efficiency.   • Move - Warehouse management & Location occupancy

    The Move (Put) process involves placing items into their designated storage locations within the warehouse after the picking process is done. The primary objective of the Move (Put) process is to efficiently allocate storage space within the warehouse to maximize capacity utilization. The system decides where to put them based on factors like available space and item type.
